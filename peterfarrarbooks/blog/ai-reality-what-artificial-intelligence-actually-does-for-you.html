AI Reality: What Artificial Intelligence Actually Does for You

There is no shortage of noise about artificial intelligence. What there is a shortage of is honesty. Every week brings another announcement, another subscription, another promise that this tool will change how you work. AI Reality strips all of that away and asks the only question that matters to a working person: does this thing actually help me, and is it worth what it costs? The book profiles ten of the leading AI assistants in plain English, ordered from the most affordable to the most expensive, so you can see exactly where the value sits before you spend a penny.

For personal users, this is the guide that saves you months of trial and error. Rather than signing up to four services and cancelling three of them, you get an honest profile of each tool, written by someone who has used them properly rather than tested them for an afternoon. You will learn which assistant is genuinely good at writing, which handles research without inventing sources, which understands images, and which is simply expensive marketing wrapped around an average product. Cost is discussed in relative terms throughout, so the guidance holds up as prices shift, and every profile is written so that a complete beginner can follow it without wading through jargon.

In the workplace the stakes are higher, and this is where AI Reality earns its place on a manager's desk. If you lead a team, your people are already using these tools whether you have approved them or not. The book covers what that means in practice: where AI genuinely reduces repetitive workload, where it introduces risk, what you should never paste into a public model, and how to have a sensible conversation with your team instead of a policy argument. There is also a standalone section warning about the use of AI for coding, written after watching confident, fluent, entirely broken output cause real problems. Managers who read this will make better decisions about tooling, training and trust.

Photographers and creatives get the same treatment. AI now touches culling, editing, retouching, cataloguing, client communication and the copy on your website, and the honest answer is that some of it is excellent and some of it will ruin your work while telling you it looks great. AI Reality shows you where the line sits, how to use these tools to protect your time without surrendering your style, and how to stay on the right side of client expectations. Whether you are running a business, leading a team, or simply trying to work out which subscription to keep, this is the book that tells you the truth rather than the pitch.
