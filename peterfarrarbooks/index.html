The story behind my leadership and photography books

Leadership expert, author, and photographer with over 30 years of experience in IT, management, and professional photography. From Senior Network Engineer at the US Senate to IT leadership in the UK defence sector — and a parallel career building a photography business in Charlotte, North Carolina.

My work is grounded in two principles: a desire to understand complex systems in depth, and a responsibility to explain them in a way that is practical and usable. Every book I write comes from direct experience — not research, not delegation, not theory. If it is in the book, I have lived it.

The photography books grew from more than a decade of professional work shooting weddings, portraits, commercial assignments, and street photography across the United States. The leadership book grew from managing teams at every level, from front-line technical roles to senior IT leadership in complex organisations including the UK defence sector. Both disciplines demand the same thing: genuine understanding, applied with discipline and care.

Writing from experience, not from research

There is a significant difference between a book written by someone who has studied a subject and one written by someone who has spent decades inside it. Every title on this site falls into the second category. The photography books were written by a working photographer who built a business from nothing in a competitive market, trained hundreds of photographers, and spent years understanding not just how cameras work but why most photographers plateau and how to break through that ceiling.

Leadership: The Culture Fix came from a career spanning IT service management, enterprise leadership, and frontline management across sectors including UK defence. It addresses the problems that most leadership books either ignore or dress up in theory, what to do when a senior manager overrides your authority, how to build genuine accountability without resorting to micromanagement, and how workplace culture actually forms and changes at the team level.

The photography series, meanwhile, covers the full journey from complete beginner to professional practice. Book one builds the creative and technical foundation. Book two goes deep on optics, sensors, and digital workflow. Book three covers the commercial and legal dimensions of running a photography business. The Complete Photography Field Guide brings all of it together in a single 538-page reference that covers both UK and US photography law, the only book of its kind to do so.
